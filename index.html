About

I'm Joe Tran, a Ph.D. student in Applied Mathematics at York University with a strong background in both pure and applied mathematics. I've spent years working with students at the high school, undergraduate, and early graduate levels, helping them not just solve problems, but actually understand the ideas behind them.

My academic training includes advanced coursework in calculus, linear algebra, probability, differential equations, measure theory, and mathematical modeling. Alongside my studies, I've worked as a teaching assistant and private tutor, where I've seen firsthand the most common reasons students struggle: gaps in foundations, unclear explanations, and a lack of structured practice.

My tutoring approach is focused on clarity and structure. I start by identifying exactly where the confusion begins, rebuild the concept step-by-step, and then reinforce it through carefully chosen examples and practice problems. I aim to make sessions efficient, supportive, and confidence-building, so students leave knowing why something works, not just how to apply a formula.

Whether you are preparing for an exam, trying to keep up with weekly coursework, or looking to strengthen your mathematical foundations, my goal is to help you feel more confident, organized, and in control of the material.
